# 002 Enterprise operating model

PBM represents a business as an operating model, not as a list of disconnected departments.

The operating model describes:

- The business profile
- The functions the business performs
- The organisation units that carry responsibility
- The positions that hold accountability
- The people assigned to those positions
- The projects, services and operational work delivered by the business
- The controls, reports and evidence used to govern the business

## Operating layers

PBM uses these operating layers:

1. Business definition
2. People and authority
3. Clients and commercial activity
4. Project and service delivery
5. Procurement and supplier cost
6. Finance and control
7. Quality, compliance and risk
8. Reporting, documents and administration

Each layer is connected through shared records.

## Ownership principle

The business owns the data spine.

A workspace owns an activity view.

A route does not own the record. It provides the right doorway for the user doing the job.

## Example

A supplier invoice can appear in multiple workspaces:

- Procurement checks whether it matches the purchase order or service received.
- Finance checks approval, due date, payment and accounting treatment.
- Project Delivery sees it as actual cost against a project or service.
- Reporting uses it for margin, cash exposure and supplier spend.

The record is shared. The activity view changes by workspace.
