# PBM Workspace Requirements

PBM workspaces are business activity areas.

They are not product modules and they do not own data.

Each workspace gives a user a practical doorway into the shared PBM data spine so that they can do a specific job against connected business objects.

## Workspace requirement structure

Each workspace requirement document uses the same structure:

1. Purpose
2. Primary users
3. Business activities
4. Business objects used
5. Data spine relationships
6. Required routes
7. Reports and controls
8. Initial exclusions

## Workspace documents

| Workspace | Requirement document | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Clients & Commercial | `clients-commercial.md` | Enquiries, opportunities, quotes, client records and accepted instructions. |
| Project Delivery | `project-delivery.md` | Instructions, projects, activities, evidence, findings, actions, deliverables and closeout. |
| People & Workforce | `people-workforce.md` | People, employees, competence, availability, assignments and utilisation. |
| Procurement & Supplier Control | `procurement-supplier-control.md` | Suppliers, purchase requests, purchase orders, subcontractors, supplier compliance and supplier cost capture. |

## Product language rule

Use PBM language:

- workspace
- capability
- activity
- business object
- route doorway
- shared data spine
- integrated record
- reporting view
- control

Do not describe these documents as modules. A module implies a separate product area with its own data. PBM is deliberately built around shared business objects and connected activity views.
